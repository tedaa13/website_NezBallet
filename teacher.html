Christiani Febriany

Known by her nickname An-an, was born in Bandung and fell in love with dance since she was a child. She joined ballet class at 2.5 years old until Senior High School. Since a young age, she has often danced at school and church. Not only ballet, but also traditional dance, modern dance, hip hop, etc.

As a professional, she often gets a chance to dance at various events such as wedding, grand opening event, festival, birthday party, etc. In 2013, she quit dancing and joined in one of the biggest TV stations in Indonesia, NET. Three years later, she got a chance to be a dancer coordinator for NET 3rd anniversary.

In March 2018, she decided to resign from NET and back to her passion, which is dancing. She joined NEZ Ballet School as an admin, then became a teacher for some ballet classes and also modern dance classes. Her dream is to join broadway, and being a part of NEZ Ballet School is just the beginning to achieve her real dream.

Gabriella Christy Sebastian Yanson

I was born on 20th December 2004, and I started Ballet at 5. I am a very cheerful girl. I would hop on my bed and sing some random songs. My father used to dance with me when I was still a little girl. In kindergarten, I attend ballet class where Miss Agnes is the teacher. Once I graduate from kindergarten, I stop dancing. After a while, I heard from Miss Agnes that she's opening her own Ballet School. From then on, I started Ballet until now.

I got dancing as talent from both of my family sides, even if my parent didn't know how to dance. I heard from my grandfather from my mom's side that he used to dance and perform in front of the Dutch. And i sometimes saw one or two pictures of my mom dancing. I also got my artistic talent from my father's side.

I love to perform in front of many audiences, but I also feel nervous even if I have practiced a lot. I'm always so excited every time we would make a gala recital, and my favorite one is when Nez Ballet School collaborates with Able Ballet. It was one of many memories I would like to remember. We went to Cirebon to practice now and then. It was honestly very exhausting but enjoyable. It was time to say goodbye to our dear friend at some point. We cried and hugged. It was such a blast. I hope I can see them again sometime in the future.

I gain a lot of experience from workshops and classes that I attend with my classmates. I learn a lot of new skills and ways to improve myself. I also get to meet many talented and beautiful people on the way. I have been friends with many people with different personalities in my ballet class. Even if some quit and I get to meet new people who joined, we aim for the best. We won't know how we will be in the future, but I hope for a bright future. It was a lot of fun dancing all these years, and I don't plan to quit. I would love to dance Ballet with my friends, and of course, I would love to do another ballet recital.

~ I'm able to go through all the hardships I face until now, so I will absolutely thrive for success. ~

Gloria

Gloria was born in Bandung and started her dance journey at the age of 4. Being a person with a curious nature, has led her to explore many dance genres since then (ranging from modern dance, tambourine, traditional, ladies, hip-hop, waacking, choreography, etc).

Gloria was born in Bandung and started her dance journey at the age of 4. Being a person with a curious nature, has led her to explore many dance genres since then (ranging from modern dance, tambourine, traditional, ladies, hip-hop, waacking, choreography, etc).

In 2018, Gloria flew to Malaysia to pursue further studies at Taylors University. At that period of time, she took CSTD Certification Program at Danztech Movement Studio and managed to finish Foundation and Level1 in Contemporary. Besides, she also actively joined routine classes for ladies style, choreography, and hip-hop that was taught by several well-known choreographer there. To mention some, Melody Tee, Ezra Tham, Fariz Azim, Dalila Samad, and many more. Some of her achievements with Danztech Team were 1st Place for Troupes Open Category in CSTD 6th Malaysia Dance Competition and 21st Asia Pacific Dance Competition 2019. During the same period, she has also won some other dance competitions with her campus team called Valerinx (Kpop) and Taylors Wildcats (Cheerleading) back then in Malaysia.

In 2020, Gloria found Ma Ladies, along with other three friends who have the same passion for ladies’ style. Since then, she has been actively involved in Maladies’s dance project and plans to do more in the future.

After completing her studies in 2021, she returned to Bandung dan teaches at 1DanceAcademy and NEZ Ballet. To date, she still joins several courses such as Senior Legacy and Dsoul Mentoring Project to expand her capacity. In addition, she is also actively creating dance video projects that she directs herself. All those long journeys have shaped Gloria into who she is today, a dancer that known for her versatility.

~ I'm dancing not because I want to. Instead, I need it to breath ~

Maria Andreana Adiesti Trishani Santosa

Born on the 16th of May,1992 in Bandung. Her passion for dance started at a very young age, since she was around 4 years old she loved to dance even made some choreography together with her cousins following the Indonesian kids songs.

She started dancing Ballet when she was 5 years old in her kindergarten at St. Angela. Her mom found that her daughter has passion in dancing, then she signed her in to Traditional Balinese dance instead of Ballet. Adies was in the 3rd grade of elementary school when she first started learned Traditional Balinese dance. She joined sanggar Githa Saraswati at Jalan Dr. Otten.

Once a year Githa Saraswati held Traditional Balinese dance recital. Every students show their dance skill after learned it in a year. Adies often being asked to perform in ant school’s event. Sometimes in wedding or in any University events.

Adies also loves modern dance. At school she also often got asked to perform dancing in many kind of events with the dance she choreographed along with her friends. It’s continue till she was in Senior High school. She made a dance group called “Miscellaneous” with her schoolmates. She stopped attend Balinese dance class due to her busy activities in college especially when she got to work.

Missing dancing life, Adies then joined Nez Ballet and Dance school in April 2015. This is a perfect place to her since here, ballet and traditional dance can be combined. She believes this dance school is a right place for students to learn not only the technical aspect of Ballet dance, but they also develop to love Indonesian traditional dances. Besides that, in this dance school students can develop their self-confidence, self-motivation and self-assessment skills through many kind of performances.

~ Dancing with the feet is one thing, but dancing with the Heart is another thing ~
